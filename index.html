Section 01Project Overview
What We're Building
A professional, conversion-focused marketing website for 180 AgPros — an agricultural consulting firm specializing in IRS Section 180 excess fertility tax deductions. The site must establish credibility, educate non-technical visitors on a complex topic, and drive qualified pre-qualification leads.
About the Business
When farmers, ranchers, or investors buy ag land, the soil already contains nutrients built up over years. Under IRS Section 180, those pre-existing nutrients can be claimed as a tax deduction — but only with proper scientific documentation. 180 AgPros provides exactly that: soil sampling, lab analysis, and CPA-ready reporting. Backed by Dr. Jake Mowrer, Ph.D., Texas A&M University.
Primary Goal
Generate pre-qualification leads from land buyers, ranchers, investors, brokers, and CPAs
Key Differentiator
Texas A&M–backed science, multi-year ongoing support, product-neutral — no fertilizer sales
Client Responsibilities
Content Provided by Client
Images, Photography & Written Content
The client is responsible for providing all website content prior to or during the build process. This includes professional photos and images (team headshots, property photos, field imagery, etc.), all written copy (service descriptions, bios, FAQs, and any other page text), and any logos or brand assets. Placeholder content may be used during development, but final content must be supplied by the client before the site can launch. Any content not provided will delay the project timeline.

Boa Safra Ag
Primary Competitor
Established brand. Strong data ($500M+ deductions generated, $1,700/acre average, 3,000+ clients). Simple 3-step process layout. Functional but visually dated. Their /get-started page is the model for 180 AgPros' Pre-Qualify page — the concept, not the design.
✦ Beat them on: modern design, polish, intuitive UX, Texas-rooted credibility with Dr. Mowrer
Soil Tax Guys
Calculator Reference
Has a basic interactive calculator for estimating Section 180 deductions. Chase wants something similar but meaningfully improved — with "what-if" scenario variables that let users explore different outcomes, plus a polished UI that captures leads at the results stage.
✦ Improve on: more input variables, cleaner output display, integrated lead capture at results
Visual Tone & Style
Overall Tone
Refined and authoritative — premium ag meets financial services. Earthy but polished, not rustic. The Texas A&M connection demands credibility. Think: Land Report magazine meets a high-end tax advisory firm.
Photography Style
Full-width aerial farmland shots, soil close-ups, professional field imagery. Minimal generic stock. Real Texas ranchland where possible. Drone-shot golden-hour fields work well as hero backgrounds.
Typography
Classic serif for headlines — conveys trust and legacy. Clean sans-serif for body copy. Bold hierarchy, generous white space. Avoid anything too startup-y or overly corporate.
